Construction

  • Site Clearance

    Site clearance is the process of removing waste as a way of making sure that an area is free from hazardous material and any other obstacles or huge mess as part of preparing the area for a construction

  • Concrete Works

    Concrete work includes the following activities: preparation of the concrete mix; transportation of the mix to the construction site; and compaction of the mix in the formwork, the curing of the concrete, etc.

  • Brick Works

    Brick works refers to the process of building with bricks.

  • Roofing

    Roofing refers to the process and materials used in constructing and covering the roof of a building.

  • Plustering

    Plustering is the process of applying a protective and decorative layer of plaster to walls, ceilings, and other surfaces.

  • Renovations

    Renovations refer to the process of improving, updating, or restoring a building or a specific part of it to make it more functional, aesthetically pleasing, or to increase its value.

  • Fencing

    Fencing in construction refers to the installation of barriers or enclosures around a property, land, or specific area to provide security, privacy, boundary definition, or aesthetic appeal.

  • Painting

    Painting in construction refers to the process of applying a protective or decorative coating of paint to surfaces such as walls, ceilings, doors, or other structural elements.

  • Plumbing

    Plumbing refers to the system of pipes, fixtures, and fittings used to distribute water, remove wastewater, and manage other fluids within a building.

  • Electrical (Wiring)

    Electrical writing refers to the technical documentation and descriptions related to electrical systems, installations, circuits, and components.
